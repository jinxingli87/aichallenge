About the Challenge

The CESASC AI Challenge brings together K-12 students, educators, mentors, and community teams across Southern California to address real-world problems using AI-powered solutions. Aligned with the Presidential AI Challenge, this regional initiative serves as a stepping stone while providing opportunities for students to connect, collaborate, and learn at the local level.

Project Tracks

Track I: Proposal

Create an in-depth proposal for applying AI technologies to address a community challenge.

Requirements:

  • 500+ word team-authored narrative
  • Presentation poster (digital or physical photo)
  • PDF submission (max 10 pages, 50 MB)
  • Address observed community challenge
  • Detail AI technology mechanisms
  • Research and justification

Track II: Technical Implementation

Build a functional AI-based solution to help address a community challenge.

Requirements:

  • 500+ word team-authored narrative
  • Demo video (max 4 minutes) or app walkthrough
  • PDF submission (max 10 pages, 50 MB)
  • Working solution (app, website, or process)
  • AI integration explanation
  • Testing and validation results

How to Participate

Assemble Your Team

Form a team of 1-5 students (grades 6-8 for Middle School or 9-12 for High School) with one Supervising Adult.

Register Your Team

The lead Educator, community group leader, or Supervising Adult should collect required consent forms and register at the Challenge website.

Select Your Track

Choose Track I (Proposal) or Track II (Technical Implementation) based on your team's goals and the problem you want to address.

Create Your Project

Develop your solution with a team-authored narrative (500+ words), plus either a presentation poster (Track I) or demo video (Track II).

Submit Deliverables

Compile everything into a single PDF (max 10 pages, 50 MB) including your narrative, links to media, and Certification of Originality.

Get Help Anytime

Email cesasc@gmail.com with any questions about your project or the process.
